Swimming

  • 500-700 calories per hour
  • Aim for atleast 30-60min per day

    • Swimming is a full-body workout that improves cardiovascular health, builds muscle strength, and enhances flexibility. It's low-impact, making it suitable for people of all ages and fitness levels.

    Did you know?

    Swimming is often recommended for individuals with arthritis or joint pain? The buoyancy of water reduces the impact on joints, providing relief while still offering a challenging workout.

    Walking

  • 200-300 calories per hour
  • 30-60min per day

    • Walking is a simple yet effective exercise that improves cardiovascular health, strengthens bones and muscles, and boosts mood and mental well-being.

    Did you know?

    Walking is often prescribed as part of cardiac rehabilitation programs due to its cardiovascular benefits and low risk of injury.

    Yoga

  • 150-300 calories per hour
  • 30-60 min per day

    • Yoga promotes flexibility, strength, and balance, while also reducing stress and improving mental focus and clarity.

    Did you know?

    Yoga has been shown to have positive effects on conditions such as hypertension, chronic pain, and anxiety disorders, making it a valuable adjunct to conventional medical treatment.
